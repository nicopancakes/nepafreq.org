What is an SDR?

An SDR (Software Defined Radio) is a radio system that uses a small piece of hardware to catch radio signals and a computer to process and understand them instead of doing everything with traditional fixed electronic parts.

Basic Idea of SDR

Traditional radios are built with special hardware that only works for certain frequencies. An SDR is much more flexible because the computer does most of the work.

The hardware simply captures raw radio signals from the air. The computer and software then turn those signals into something you can hear or see.

How an SDR Works

  • An antenna picks up radio signals from the air
  • The SDR device converts those signals into digital data
  • The computer processes the data using special software
  • The software shows you the audio, images, or other information

What Can I Receive Using an SDR?

Local Signals

  • FM and AM radio stations
  • Air traffic control conversations
  • Aircraft positions using ADS-B
  • Weather satellite images
  • Ship movements and marine signals

Shortwave and HF Signals

  • International broadcast stations from around the world
  • Amateur (ham) radio operators talking across countries
  • Long-distance aviation and maritime communications
  • Time and frequency standard signals

SDR vs Handheld Radios

  • Handheld radios are mainly used for two-way voice communication
  • SDR is designed for listening to and exploring many different signals
  • SDR shows you a visual display of the entire radio spectrum
  • Handheld radios work on their own without a computer

Important Concepts

  • Frequency – The specific “channel” where a signal lives
  • Bandwidth – How wide or narrow a signal is
  • Noise Floor – The background level of radio noise
  • Antenna Quality – A good antenna makes a big difference in what you can hear
